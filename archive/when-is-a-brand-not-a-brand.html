When is a brand not a brand?

23rd Nov 2015

James "Midge" Ure, OBE

This morning I listened to this world class rock legend chat about brands and what he has recently discovered about them.

The fascinating thing for me and for many in the brand world, is how few famous people know they are a brand. Brands are like people and great people are brands in their own right...they become a brand slowly but surely as we fall in love with them.

 

His Values

Midge, told us a lovely story about how, throughout his career, he has stuck to his values (apart from once, which he regrets - I think it still haunts him) and how this has helped him steer a course and live a life he is proud of. Now, don’t get me wrong, he is still learning, and still experiencing situations where he is challenged, both about brand and about what he personally stands for. He mentioned some easy decisions he made when his daughters were born to shelve the high flying world of rock and focus on his home life. He has four daughters and reckons "God has been getting him back for his early years of playing the field with all the young girls - he now knows the torment the young girls go though”.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Band Aid

You can’t help feel, when he talks about the other brands he helped create on the world stage, he centres his life around doing as much good for the less fortunate as possible. Not only did he and many other top artists give so much to helping Ethiopia in 1984, he continues to do just that in many aspects of his life. This is what makes this brand proud.

He mentions the times he nearly went bust because he did the 'right thing’ very often not the commercial thing. He also suggests the great things we do start with the emotion then get solved by the brain. We will feel something first, and then it needs a solid bit of objective thinking to make sure it can be solved well and continue to be solved beyond the first fix.

For us in the branding world, a brand is only a brand when it is loved, has fantastic values which it sticks to through thick and thin and lives beyond the people who started it or the people who live it.

George McRae
