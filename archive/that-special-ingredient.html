Put a little passion in your brand

When Levi Roots arrived relaxed, singing and strumming his guitar into the cauldron that is the BBC’s Dragons Den in 2012, the slightly bemused ‘Dragons’ were immediately captivated by Levi as a personality.   Despite some lack of business acumen and misguided understanding of the figures, usually critical to achieving an investment, Levi Roots conveyed love, excitement and sheer passion for his product, Reggae Reggae Sauce.  As the other Dragons were announcing the dreaded ‘I’m out’, millionaire investor Peter Jones declared ‘I like  impossible challenges’ and offered 50% of the £50,000 requested funds, galvanising a partner Dragon to jump in.

 

Shortly after Levi’s appearance on TV, Sainsbury’s announced they would be stocking the sauce in 600 of its UK stores and today the repackaged branded sauce and product extensions can be found in many of the major supermarkets.  In 2015, Levi Roots is estimated to be worth £30 million and one of Dragons Den’s most successful contestants.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Passion brands are those where consumers have an emotional connection, buying into the brand personality.  In some organisations this may be fronted and driven by individuals such as Levi Roots or Richard Branson, however to really make an impact the brand personality will be at the heart of all touch points.  How the brand speaks to the customer, the messages, design, product, innovation and the customer journey are all key ingredients.

 

Engaging, exciting and continually surprising the customer are attributes that passion global brands such as Apple or Nike have been phenomenal at.  Positioning themselves as cool, innovators and through great design, they lead their competitors and have the world talking as they launch new products.  It was reported that Apple sold 10 million units within 3 days of the launch of iPhone 6 and Nike’s sponsorship of major sporting superstars such as Cristiano Ronaldo and Rafael Nadal has no doubt contributed towards their position at number 18 of the world’s most valuable brands in 2015.*

 

As a happy, regular consumer of Reggae Reggae sauce, I often wonder – the Dragons that said ‘I’m out’ back in 2012, how frequently have they regretted not engaging the Passion in the Den?

Fiona Melvin-Farr
