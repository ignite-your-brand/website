John Lewis Christmas Advert 2015:

https://youtu.be/wuz2ILq4UeA

Experiencing the young girl’s wonder, as she sees a man on the moon through her father’s telescope one night, going about his chores all alone. We appreciate her childlike determination to get a message to the man in the moon to let him know he’s not alone. The sound track (‘Half the World Away’ performed by Aurora) pulls at our heart strings.

We are invited to continue the story.

At first the exact story is not clear because there are so many messages here, all combing into a symphony that optimizes the wholeness of the John Lewis Brand, their vision, mission and values. The overall message is clearly that we care about everyone, everywhere. It does  not matter who you are.

Lets look at the values that John Lewis has built its brand on:

Making better decisions together – sharing the responsibility and the success. 'Employee Ownership, A Shared Passion'. The little girl takes it on herself to get a message to the man in the moon.

Using our profits to grow sustainably - to be commercially viable to look after all stakeholders, including community. The affluence and togetherness of Christmas is emphasized alongside the sense of aloneness that people can feel. The story tells there is a gift for everyone that can be delivered with the determination. Behind this video are a whole range of man in the moon merchandise for xmas gifts, including the music and the promotion of any other products that fit under the man in the moon theme which can be delivered by John Lewis home delivery service.

 

​

 

Generating loyalty through choice, value and service – looking after the customers best interests and across all range of prices and gifts for all.

Leading with a more engaging approach - to attract people with integrity and a sense of contribution, valuing differences and being fair. The video plays across all of us from the very young to the very old and all part of the family on planet earth, even those that are far away from us. This theme is continued through donations to age concern from the advertising campaign and to kids’ education in relation to astrology and science, Creating value beyond the Partnership for community.

An organisation's mission, vision and values
should be conjured up by the brand and
provide a key insight into the strategy
and culture of a business.

Man in the Moon, looking back on oneself through the telescope, on ones journey in life on starship planet earth, is a joint journey that can only successfully be achieved through being part of a caring community no matter what age or role we play. John Lewis Cares… continue the story with us…

Do you feel compelled to do so?

Mel Harris

Man on the moon
