Well, I suppose it depends on how badly they cheat?, what they did, and how important the friendship or connection is to you. I mean, if they slept with your husband or wife, that would be fairly detrimental to your relationship, some would say untenable. Usually our friendships are based on some core values, and usually one of the most vital of them is trust... and when broken, it’s not typically a quick fix.

It is fair to say, most of us engage with brands in much the same way. They reflect our beliefs and how we would like to be seen by our friends, family and peers. More and more - due to a massive choice in many cases - we are selecting the brands we feel are good for us and help to say something about us as individuals.

 ‘If only everything was as reliable as a Volkswagen’

Imagine one of the worlds most trusted brands cheating. Imagine the consequences when they are an international brand who’s core values include ‘safe’ ‘trustworthy’ and ‘reliable', suddenly cheat?. What would it do to the fiscal value?, immediately?, and how would this in turn affect the long term view of it or how long would it take to gain that trust back? What would you have to do? and for how long? And would we trust anything they said ever again?

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

The latest news with just such a brand continues to rock the car world and undermine the value of the brand; "Monday's announcement broadens the scandal to SUVs, including the 3-liter diesel engine versions of the 2014 Volkswagen Touareg, the 2015 Porsche Cayenne and the 2016 Audi Q5."

Unlike the initial violation, in which VW admitted culpability, this time it says it inserted no software to trick emissions testing equipment in the latest models. VW said in a statement that it "wishes to emphasise that no software has been installed in the 3-litre V-6 diesel power units to alter emissions characteristics in a forbidden manner."  A VW spokesman, Mario Guerreiro, said he would not elaborate on the statement, including whether it means VW is denying that it cheated by any means.

It's yet to be seen if the latest allegation damages VW's reputation further, and how deeply or for how long. We cannot understand why something as fundamental as trust was allowed to be broken in the first place. Maybe the values stopped in the boardroom and failed to reach the whole company. Or maybe the whole company wanted to do the right thing and the top team pushed their agenda for the sake of the stock price?. Either way, in our view, it was a gamble which is now going to cost them far more than it could ever have gained, and could wreck the value of the brand for a very long time.

George McRae
