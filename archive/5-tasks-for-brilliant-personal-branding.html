Personal branding was first introduced in 1937 in the book Think and Grow Rich by Napoleon Hill. Hill states, “It should be encouraging to know that practically all the great fortunes began in the form of compensation for personal services, or from the sale of IDEAS.”  (Napoleon Hill researched more than forty millionaires to find out what made them the men they were.)

 

1. Think of yourself as a brand
In what way do you wish people to associate with you when they think of your name? What subject are you the expert in, what qualities do you wish people to know you for? Understanding how you wish your brand to be perceived is the start to being much more strategic about how you present yourself. Authenticity is key in the digital age; a strong personal brand can yield a great return-on-investment whether you are working with an organisation or leading one. Here are some examples of individuals who have built up authentic and powerful personal brands:

Zoella
Richard Branson
Seth Godon
Gary Vaynerchuk

2. Google your name!
Okay, so you’ve done that already. How about setting up Google Alerts? Make sure you get an email for every time someone mentions your name online. Now go through your search and audit those images and stories. The best way to push unwanted content is to create lots of new more topical content, which leads me on to:

3. Your personal website
I hear that more and more it is becoming a Christmas present, buying and securing your own domain name. Don’t be proud, it’s not a state of arrogance it is ensuring that your legacy is documented in a way that you wish to be presented. The alternative is using something like http://about.me or http://jimdo.com

Everything online is now mobile first, so ensure that the template or format you choose to present your website is mobile friendly.

4. Sharing what??
Where do you start… consider the things that you find invaluable, those tools, the advice, the insights and methodology that have taken you to where you are now. Reveal the case-studies; ensure you use plenty of images and footage. As for your CV…don’t (unless of course you’re job hunting), providing a link to your LinkedIn profile is sufficient, it should be written for who you want to attract not the tasks you carried out in each role.

5. Brand allegiance
As with any brand, consider carefully who you are happy to be associated with. Create a list of people and brands you are happy to be aligned with and consider how you might either work with them or include them in content you write. Write knowledgeably about comparable subject matters and integrate their point of view. Reach out to them and ask for their input on a subject you’d like to know more about.

Share some great ideas.
